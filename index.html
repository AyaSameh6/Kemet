1. Fjord Bay, Taba

Nestled within the heart of the Sinai Peninsula, the Fjord Bay in Taba, Egypt is a hidden gem that offers an unforgettable scenery. This bay, a fjord in its own right, is a testament to the grandeur of natureâ€™s creations. It is located in the eastern part of Egypt, where the Sinai Peninsula meets the Red Sea. This location is a paradise for both experienced divers and those who simply seek a tranquil escape.

The Fjord Bay is a unique blend of a fjord and a bay, characterized by its breathtakingly beautiful beaches and the open sea. The bay is a haven for those who want to experience the beauty of the Red Sea in a less crowded setting. Its turquoise water and golden sand beaches are framed by rugged mountains, creating a picturesque landscape that is truly unforgettable.

2. Nuweiba, Gulf of Aqaba
The city is famous for being called the jewel of the Gulf as Nuweiba has wonderful golden beaches surrounded by many palm trees; giving the city special magic of its own. Nuweiba beach is located in South Sinai within Nueiba, which makes it not only easily accessible, but even popular among tourists. also, Between the town and the port are a strip of modern hotels, catering to beach holiday makers and divers. One km north of Nuweiba City, Tarabin village is well known for its Bedouin-style camps where cheap huts are available for rent. Further north, in the direction of Taba, are several other beaches with similar accommodation options. Nuweiba is known for some of its popular attractions, which include:
  1. Coloured Canyon
  2. Ain Khudra
  3. Nawamis
  4. Coral Island
  5. Abu Hamada Canyon
3. Blue Lagoon, Dahab
visit to the Blue Lagoon is still easily one of the best thing to do in dahab! This lagoon is a large sheltered natural pond of calm, crystal-clear water. You'll find it sheltered by a low, arching stretch of sand and rock, protecting it from the wild sea of the Gulf of Aqaba. Along its shores, you'll find small local restaurants and many small huts owned by the local Bedouins of Dahab and the surrounding Sinai Peninsula. This is a perfect place to spend the day relaxing, swimming, or exploring the surrounding area. Also, due to its natural beauty, calm ambiance, and steady waters, the Blue Lagoon is a kitesurfing paradise. I didn't visit for kitesurfing but it was awesome to watch the pros from the shore! some Activities you could do:
  1. Hike
  2. camel ride
  3. speedboat to Abu Galoum
